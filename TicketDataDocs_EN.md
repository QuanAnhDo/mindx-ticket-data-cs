# STRATEGIC REPORT: BUILDING A TECHNICAL SUPPORT KNOWLEDGE HUB

## 1. CURRENT STATUS & URGENT NEEDS
Currently, technical support data (Tickets) faces several challenges:
- **Fragmented Data:** Troubleshooting content is scattered across emails, making it difficult to reference when similar issues recur.
- **Onboarding Difficulties:** New CS or TechTeam members spend significant time re-learning existing business processes (e.g., handling payment errors, LMS enrollment issues).
- **Lack of Systemization:** Solutions are often temporary and have not been distilled into standardized guides.

## 2. GITBOOK KNOWLEDGE HUB STRATEGY
Instead of building complex software tools, this phase focuses on **Information Architecture** using the **GitBook** platform:

### 2.1. Why GitBook?
- **Semantic Search:** Users can simply type an issue (e.g., "cannot submit assignment"), and the system suggests relevant articles.
- **Scientific Categorization:** Allows organizing folders by business domains (LMS, CRM, Finance, Operations).
- **Collaboration:** The entire team can easily contribute to, edit, and enrich Markdown content.

## 3. INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE (CONTENT STRUCTURE)
Each knowledge article (Ticket) will be standardized according to the following structure:
1.  **Title:** A brief summary of the issue.
2.  **Categorization (Tags):** Labels for related systems (#LMS, #Finance, #Enroll).
3.  **Phenomenon:** A detailed description of the problem encountered by the customer.
4.  **Solution:** Specific steps to resolve the issue.
5.  **Business Notes:** Explanations of why the error occurred to prevent recurrence.

## 4. OPERATIONAL WORKFLOW
The project will follow a 4-step data standardization process:
1.  **Collection:** Aggregating representative tickets from Emails.
2.  **Standardization:** Removing redundant exchanges and keeping only core business logic and solutions.
3.  **Categorization:** Placing articles into the correct GitBook categories and applying tags to optimize search.
4.  **Review:** TechTeam periodically reviews and updates information as system processes change.

## 5. PRIORITIZATION (MOSCOW MODEL)
To ensure the project stays on track and optimizes resources, items are prioritized as follows:

- **MUST-HAVE:**
    - Establish a standard category framework on GitBook (LMS, CRM, Finance).
    - Complete statistics for the most common troubleshooting guides.
    - Ensure the search functionality works accurately on GitBook.

- **SHOULD-HAVE:**
    - Synchronized tagging system for rapid data filtering (#Finance, #Enroll).
    - Style Guide for consistent and easy-to-understand content.
    - Access permissions for personnel based on business groups.

- **COULD-HAVE:**
    - Short illustrative videos for complex handling processes.
    - Feedback system for users to rate article usefulness.
    - Business summaries for each related group of issues.

- **WON'T-HAVE:**
    - Full automation of article creation (Focusing on manual/semi-automatic content standardization first).
    - Custom UI (Leveraging GitBook's professional default interface).
